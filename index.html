Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

Early innovation set the stage for long-term improvements in efficiency — doing more computing with less energy over time.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

Smaller chips = less material and power. Miniaturization is one path toward sustainability.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

Performance-per-watt matters: better computing without huge energy increases.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

More efficient architectures reduce energy used per task as computing demand grows.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

A turning point: investing in clean energy + improved manufacturing processes.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Sustainability isn't one action — it's a strategy with measurable goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040.

Long-term targets help guide short-term improvements and accountability.

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions.

Renewables reduce operational emissions and support cleaner manufacturing.

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

Collaboration is key — sustainability progress happens across the whole supply chain.
